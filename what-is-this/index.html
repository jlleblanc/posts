What is this?

I have a long history with the World Wide Web. It sounds mainly like the history most people from my age, background, and privilege have with it, so that will be something I share another time.

I've gone from having a PHP rendered site, to a CMS driven site, to blogs, to flat one-page sites, to JAM-stack sites and now have landed back on flat HTML/CSS pages.

Through all of that, I picked up Markdown and started using that wherever I was authoring content. Markdown made sense for the most part. It keeps you focused on writing and basic structure, while ensuring you never wind up with malformed HTML. I generally avoided writing CSS whenever possible, accepting whatever defaults wiser designers chose for my websites.

"I'm not a journalist, my work isn't news, I'm not getting paid to do this, and I don't care to moderate incoming comments."

However, I was getting bored with this. I found myself working on all of these experiments with JavaScript and JSON, looking for new ways to express ideas and share them. In the meantime, CSS was still sitting there largely unused by me. Even many designers I know have moved on from web design, CSS and all.

Another thing I realized was that blogging and social media posting are limited by design. I've been looking for ways to express my ideas in some form other than reverse-chronological. I also want to avoid authoring things with algorithms in mind. I'm not a journalist, my work isn't news, I'm not getting paid to do this, and I don't care to moderate incoming comments.

Finally, I've realized that while having the limits of Markdown may be helpful in some cases, they're not so helpful in others. When you're handing a website over to someone to maintain on their own without HTML/CSS knowledge, Markdown can be very useful. But if you're trying to experiment and continue learning HTML/CSS, Markdown can get in the way.

I've also discovered the amazing CSS editing tools in Firefox! These tools make it easy to see your changes transition in real-time, as well as save your changes back to the original CSS files.

This site is a guarantee-free, experimental zone of ideas and pages. I may use JavaScript at some point or I may never add it at all. This may be the last post I write here or I may keep going for years to come.
