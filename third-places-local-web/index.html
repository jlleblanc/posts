Establishing Digital Third Places in a Person-Centric Local Web

Workshop description

The current World Wide Web is a collection of corporate-centric platforms where people are incentivized to generate and post attention-seeking content. Trust is low, misinformation is prevalent, and the volume is overwhelming. What if we built a high-trust, low-noise, person-centric, Local Web? Let's take a look at how we could build a Local Web of high-trust digital "third places" to serve friends, family, neighbors, and local organizations. We will discuss the values that a Local Web should have and also connect to an example server using standard web browsers.

Values

Welcome

Participants should feel welcome and included as they join a Local Web. As participants are welcomed to a Local Web, they should extend the same welcome and inclusion to others present. A Code of Conduct should be readily accessible and applied to everyone equally.

Self-Determination

Aside from a name, personal profiles should have no required data. Participants in a Local Web are free to represent themselves however they wish. The name given does not need to be a legal name. Also, the same person can use different profiles for different Local Web networks.

Authenticity

Joining a Local Web should be a deliberate action. Participants should ensure the Local Web they are joining is authentic before presenting their name or profile. Similarly, if you are the steward of a Local Web, you should take care to only admit real people.

Decentralization

Communication over a Local Web should happen directly between participants. While the establishment of a Local Web may need some level of centralized coordination (e.g., a conventional WWW site for directory assistance), Local Webs should be independent of each other. One Local Web should not rely on content shared in another Local Web.

Privacy

A Local Web is not automatically available to the entire public world. While participants may join a Local Web through a public venue (e.g., coffeeshop, non-profit organization, etc…), that venue may not necessarily receive everyone who attempts to join. Participants must be a part of a specific Local Web before accessing the data within it. Data within the Local Web is not published elsewhere.

    • Now let's hear from _____ and ____ when you are done, please pick the next person to share!
  • Thank you! I think that's everyone, did we miss anyone? ... Great!

Third Places

  • Thanks again for introducing yourselves and your favorite places! The places you shared are examples of "third places."
  • The term "third place" is used by sociologists and urban planners to refer to a place that is outside of your home, school, or workplace that you visit on a regular basis.
  • The "first place" is your home, the "second place" is your workplace or school, and "third places" are found throughout your town or city.
  • Some examples of third places include
    • parks
    • public squares
    • cafes
    • coffeeshops
    • hairstylist shops (like salons and barbershops)
    • houses of worship
  • These third places are vital for our social health for many reasons.
    • They give us a break from the places where we spend the majority of our time; a "home away from home"
    • All are welcome: they introduce us to people we might not otherwise interact with
    • They make it possible for us to be ourselves (ex. LGBTQ+ coffeeshop)

Digital Third Spaces

  • With the ongoing pandemic, we have been separated from many of our physical third spaces and have turned to digital ones.
  • Here are some examples:
    • Message boards on platforms such as Facebook
    • Group texts
    • Video games
    • Video chats, similar to the one were in right now!
  • Some of these digital third spaces have been well designed, while others have been put together hastily.
  • Also, some of these spaces serve the profit interests of corporations more than our social well-being.
  • We see this through the abuse of algorithms for advertising purposes.

My goals for this workshop

Given the current situation with digital third spaces, I have three goals for this workshop today:

  1. Help you get the most out of the digital third spaces you are already a part of.
  2. Help you establish new digital third spaces that serve our social health.
  3. Recruit you to a Slack group to help me with a project I call the Local Web!
  • The Local Web is my early-stage project for making protocols and tools to establish new digital third spaces.
  • It is driven by a set of values, which we will be discussing in breakout groups now.

Local Web Values

  • The values of the Local Web project are centered around our social needs for inclusion and safety.
  • Here are a few of the ones I've considered:
    • Welcome
    • Self-determination
    • Authenticity
    • Decentralization
    • Privacy

Breakout Groups

  • In a moment, you will get an invitation to join a breakout room.
  • In this room, you will be discussing one of these five values.
  • I am asking that in each group, one person volunteer for each of these roles:
    • Timekeeper: make sure the group is not running out of time
    • Note taker: take notes on the discussion using ____ (hopefully Miro)
    • Moderator: make sure everyone has a chance to participate
    • Reporter: summarize takeaways and share for 1-2 minutes when we reconvene
  • We should have 4-5 people per breakout group.
  • If you find you have fewer than 4 people in your group, come back here to the main room where I can assist.
  • However, please wait a full minute after joining the breakout to make sure everyone has had a chance to get in.
  • Does anyone have any questions? ... Good! The breakout rooms are starting now and will run for ____ minutes.

Questions for the breakout groups

Please use these questions as a guide for your discussion and reflections. You do not necessarily have to answer every question.

  • How have you seen this value in any of the digital third spaces you are a part of?
  • How could your current digital third spaces be improved by this value?
  • What are some of the challenges with implementing this value?
  • How do corporation-owned platforms (e.g. Facebook, TikTok) help or hinder this value?

Regroup

  • Alright everyone, welcome back! If your group is still summarizing, let me know and we can have another group report while you are finishing up.
