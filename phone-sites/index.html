Phone Sites

Direct dialing over the public telephone network is something we take for granted. Up until the 1950's, all phone calls were manually connected by operators. When this changed, each country devised their dialing plans (including things like area codes), which made local phone numbers easy to remember. Of course, these are not as easy to remember as domain names, but everyone with a phone got a phone number.

Phones in the United States did not change all that much for the next 30 years or so. Touch-tone dialing was introduced, but that was about it.

Then changes started happening quickly.

The phone company was broken up as a part of an antitrust lawsuit. Where before phone equipment had to be rented from the phone company, any equipment could now be connected to the phone line.
